My Journey from Hair Salon to Developer

Two years ago, I was a beautician working at a busy salon, cutting and styling hair 10 hours a day. While I loved making people feel beautiful, I felt creatively limited and knew the physical demands wouldn't be sustainable long-term. I had zero coding experience and honestly found technology intimidating.

One evening, after standing on my feet all day, I was scrolling through my phone and stumbled across a free coding tutorial. Something clicked. The logical, creative problem-solving reminded me of the artistry I loved about hair design, but for the digital world.

The journey wasn't easy. I studied every morning before opening the salon, practiced during my lunch breaks, and coded after my last client. There were moments I wanted to quit, especially when I couldn't understand basic concepts that seemed "obvious" to others.

But I persisted. I built projects, contributed to open source, and slowly built confidence. After 6 intense months of self-directed learning while working full-time, I landed my first developer role at a startup.

Today, I work as a full-stack developer, earn more than I ever thought possible, and absolutely love what I do. More importantly, I've proven to myself that anyone can make this transition with the right guidance and mindset.
